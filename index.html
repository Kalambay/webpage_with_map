Introduction

This webpage is providing visualized information about the bike sharing system in Washington DC. The data was captured from https://www.capitalbikeshare.com. By analyzing and visualizing those data, we are trying to answer questions such as: How the demand for bikes in DC changes during a normal weekday on an hourly basis; From where most bike rides usually start; What destination the bike users are usually travelling to. The final goal of this analysis is to assess the overall performance of the sharing bike system in Washington DC, and propose possible solutions to improve the efficiency of the whole system so as to enhance users experience.

Conclusion

This analysis explores the bike sharing system visualized in Washington DC using data, barcharts, heatmap and connection map. Our study has many findings. Firstly, the bike pickups appear to be higher during the weekdays than during the weekends in January. Second, the bikes are more used for short trips in dense urban areas (which can be seen in the connection map with dense urban areas). For the longer trips beyond the metropolitan area, and so few. Thirdly, bikes are used both for commuting and for recreation. Finally, the use of shared bikes is seen in the city along the metro line, which indicates that people choose to use bikes to ride to nearby metro stations and use bikes to travel nearby after heading by metro from one part of the city to another.
